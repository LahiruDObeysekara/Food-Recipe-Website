Achcharu

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of a mix of vegetables and fruits (carrots, green mango, pineapple, cucumber, etc.), finely julienned or grated
  • 1 medium-sized red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2-3 green chilies, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 cup of white vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2-3 tablespoons vegetable oil

Procedure

  1. Prepare the Vegetables and Fruits: Julienne or grate the mix of vegetables and fruits (carrots, green mango, pineapple, cucumber, etc.). Set aside.
  2. Slice Onion and Chilies: Thinly slice the red onion and slice the green chilies.
  3. Prepare the Achcharu Base: In a mixing bowl, combine the julienned or grated vegetables and fruits, sliced red onion, sliced green chilies, and minced garlic.
  4. Prepare the Achcharu Sauce: In a small saucepan, combine sugar, white vinegar, chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves. Let it come to a gentle boil and then remove it from the heat.
  5. Mix Sauce with Vegetables and Fruits: Pour the prepared sauce over the mix of vegetables and fruits. Mix well, ensuring that the sauce coats all the ingredients evenly.
  6. Temper with Vegetable Oil: In a separate small pan, heat vegetable oil. Once hot, pour it over the mixed Achcharu and stir well.
  7. Let it Marinate: Allow the Sinhala Achcharu to marinate for at least a few hours or overnight in the refrigerator. This helps the flavors to meld together.
  8. Serve: Sinhala Achcharu is ready to be served! Enjoy it as a spicy and tangy accompaniment to your meals.
