Hoppers

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups raw rice
  • 1/2 cup cooked rice
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 1/2 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • Salt, to taste

Procedure:

  1. Soak and Grind Rice: Wash the raw rice thoroughly and soak it in water for about 4-6 hours or overnight. In a blender, combine the soaked raw rice, cooked rice, grated coconut, and enough water to make a smooth batter.
  2. Ferment the Batter: Add the active dry yeast, sugar, and a pinch of salt to the batter. Blend again to mix. Transfer the batter to a bowl, cover it, and let it ferment for about 6-8 hours or overnight. The batter should rise and develop a slightly sour aroma.
  3. Prepare the Batter: After fermentation, add baking soda and salt to the batter. Mix well.
  4. Cooking Hoppers: Heat an appam pan or a small wok over medium heat. Grease it lightly with oil. Pour a ladle of batter into the center of the pan, and quickly swirl the pan to spread the batter thinly, coating the sides to form a bowl shape. Cover the pan with a lid and let the hopper cook until the edges become crispy, and the center is soft and spongy.
  5. Serve: Carefully remove the hopper from the pan and repeat the process with the remaining batter. Serve hoppers warm with your favorite accompaniments such as coconut sambol, curry, or chutney.
